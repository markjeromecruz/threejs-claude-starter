# 💡 Prompt Idea Book

Copy any line below, paste it to Claude, and watch your world change. Every
prompt here is sized to be **one comfortable step** — visible in the game within
a couple of minutes.

New here? Do the setup in **[START-HERE.md](START-HERE.md)** first.

**How to use this book:** one idea per message. Change the words to make it
yours — colors, numbers, names, and "make it weirder" are all encouraged. The
kid's own words always beat these.

---

## 🌟 First wins (start here — instantly visible, can't go wrong)

- *Make the floating crystals rainbow-colored, and add five more.*
- *Make it sunset — pink and orange sky.*
- *Make the crystals bounce higher, like they're excited.*
- *Change the game's title to "[your game's name]".*
- *Make the grass a deeper green and add way more trees around the edges.*

## 📱 Play on a tablet or phone

The starter moves with the keyboard only, so on a touch screen you can look
around but not walk. One prompt fixes it:

- *Add an on-screen joystick so I can move around on a tablet or phone.*
- *Add big touch buttons for jump and interact, for playing on a tablet.*

- *Add a little pond with lily pads.*
- *Put a snowy mountain far away in the distance.*
- *Add mushrooms that glow in different colors.*
- *Surround the courtyard with a cozy wooden fence with a gate.*
- *Add falling autumn leaves drifting through the air.*

## 🧍 Characters & creatures

- *Add a friendly character standing by the fountain. When I click them, they
  say hello.*
- *Let me choose a character and walk around AS them, instead of a floating
  camera.*
- *Make the character wander around on their own when I'm not controlling them.*
- *Add a wizard who slowly walks in a circle around the fountain.*
- *Give my character a jump when I press the space bar.*

## 🐾 Animals & pets

- *Add a pet dog that follows me around.*
- *Let me throw a ball and have the dog fetch it.*
- *Add birds that fly across the sky sometimes.*
- *Put ducks on the pond that paddle around.*
- *Add a cat sleeping on the fountain edge that wakes up when I get close.*

## 🎮 Make it a game

- *Make the crystals collectible — when I touch one, it disappears with a
  sparkle and my score goes up.*
- *Show my score at the top of the screen.*
- *Add coins scattered around the world that I can collect.*
- *Add a treasure chest that opens when I click it.*
- *Add a shop where I can spend my coins.*
- *Give me a quest: an NPC asks me to find their 3 lost gems.*
- *Add levels — every 10 coins, show "LEVEL UP!" with confetti.*

## 🌙 Day, night & weather

- *Add a day/night cycle — the sun slowly sets, stars come out, then it rises
  again.*
- *Make the lanterns actually glow at night.*
- *Add a full moon that lights everything softly at night.*
- *Sometimes make it rain, with puddle sounds of… okay, just the rain.* 🌧️
- *Add fireflies that only come out in the evening.*

## 🏠 Building the neighborhood

- *Add a little house with a door I can walk up to.*
- *Give the house an inside I can walk into, with a bed and a table.*
- *Add a park with swings and a slide.*
- *Add a campfire we can sit around, with crackling flames.*
- *Put balloons and a "Happy Birthday" banner on the house.* 🎈

## 🤪 Silly & delightful

- *Make everything HUGE for 10 seconds when I press B.*
- *Add a button that makes it rain donuts.*
- *Give the fountain rainbow water.*
- *Make a secret: if I click the biggest tree 3 times, something magical
  happens (surprise us!).*
- *Add a dance move when I press D.*

## 🕹️ Mini-games (bigger asks — Claude will build them in steps)

- *Add a hide-and-seek game where a creature hides and I have to find it.*
- *Add a race track with a countdown and a timer.*
- *Add an arcade machine in the corner that plays a tiny game when clicked.*

## 📜 The real journey (for inspiration)

This template is the *actual first file* of a real family's game. Below is the
real order of ideas that grew it from the courtyard you have now into a whole
living town — **every line was once a single ask from a kid**:

1. Put our own characters in the world
2. Make them roam around the courtyard
3. Click a character to make it talk
4. Choose a character and walk around as them
5. Add collectible stars
6. Add a store with a shopkeeper, and spend your stars shopping
7. Add a dressing room — buy and wear accessories
8. Add a touch-screen joystick so it works on a tablet
9. Give every character their own house
10. Make the houses two stories with furniture inside
11. Add stairs you can actually climb
12. Add a day/night cycle and bedtime routines
13. Add a hospital, a doctor, and an ambulance
14. Add a campfire with marshmallows
15. Add a pet dog that follows you
16. Throw a ball for the dog to fetch
17. Name the puppy and give her a doghouse
18. Party balloons on the houses, and music
19. Make the moon light up the whole town at night (softly)
20. Add neighborhood families and a park
21. Save the game, and add a start menu
22. Add quests, coins, and leveling up
23. Add a duck pond, a garden you can grow, and a playground
24. Add fishing, crops, and arcade machines with mini-games inside

Total time: a few weekends. Total code written by the family: **zero**. They
described; Claude built; GitHub published. Your list will look completely
different — and that's the whole point. ✨

## 🎯 Leveling up your asks

Once you're rolling, better prompts = better results:

- **Add numbers and colors:** "add coins" → *"add 20 gold coins that spin
  slowly, spread around the whole map."*
- **Describe the feeling:** *"make the night feel cozy, not spooky."*
- **Ask Claude to brainstorm with you:** *"what are 5 fun things we could add
  to the park?"* — then pick one.
- **Ask "why" when curious:** *"what did you change to make the dog follow me?"*
  Claude explains beautifully, and it's a sneaky great way to start learning
  real coding. 🧠
